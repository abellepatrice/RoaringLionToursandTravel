Serengeti National Park, located in northern Tanzania, is renowned for its vast savannahs, stunning landscapes, and incredible wildlife. It is best known for hosting the annual Great Migration, where millions of wildebeest, zebras, and other animals traverse the plains in search of water and grazing lands.


Activities in Serengeti National Park

Game Drives: Explore the park on guided safari game drives to witness the abundant wildlife, including lions, elephants, giraffes, cheetahs, and more.
Balloon Safaris: Experience the Serengeti from a unique perspective by taking a hot air balloon safari, offering breathtaking views of the landscapes and wildlife below.
Guided Nature Walks: Join experienced guides on nature walks to discover the park's diverse ecosystems, spot smaller animals and birds, and learn about the flora and fauna.
Photography Tours: Capture stunning images of the Serengeti's wildlife and landscapes, with opportunities for close-up shots of animals in their natural habitat.
Cultural Visits: Learn about the local Maasai culture with visits to nearby villages, where you can interact with Maasai tribespeople, learn about their traditions, and witness traditional dances.
Bush Dinners: Enjoy a memorable dining experience under the African sky with bush dinners arranged by safari camps and lodges, accompanied by traditional entertainment.

Wildlife in Serengeti National Park

Lions: Serengeti is known for its large lion population, with prides often spotted lounging on the plains or hunting their prey.
Elephants: These majestic giants can be seen wandering through the savannah, often in family groups.
Giraffes: Towering giraffes gracefully roam the Serengeti, feeding on acacia leaves and providing excellent photo opportunities.
Cheetahs: Watch these fast and agile predators in action as they hunt across the open plains of the Serengeti.
Wildebeest and Zebras: Witness the iconic Great Migration, where millions of wildebeest and zebras move in search of fresh grazing grounds, attracting predators along the way.
Rhinos: While less common, both black and white rhinos can still be found in certain areas of the park, contributing to conservation efforts.
Hippos and Crocodiles: Spot these aquatic creatures in the rivers and waterholes of the Serengeti, particularly during the dry season.
Birdlife: Serengeti is also home to a rich diversity of bird species, making it a paradise for birdwatchers.

Best Time to Visit

Dry Season (June to October):

This period offers excellent wildlife viewing opportunities, as animals gather around water sources and vegetation is less dense, making sightings easier. The weather is typically dry and mild, with cooler temperatures in the mornings and evenings.

Wet Season (November to May):

The wet season brings lush green landscapes and newborn wildlife, but it can also mean muddy roads and increased vegetation, making wildlife sightings more challenging. However, this is a great time for birdwatching, as migratory birds flock to the area.

Shoulder Seasons (March to May and November):

These transitional months offer a balance between the dry and wet seasons, with fewer tourists and still plenty of wildlife to see. It's a good time to visit for those looking to avoid crowds and enjoy pleasant weather.
