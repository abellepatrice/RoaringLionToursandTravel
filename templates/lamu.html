Lamu

Lamu is a small island off the north-east coast of Kenya. It is the oldest living Swahili town south of the sahara, and this has earned it recognition as a world heritage site by UNESCO. The small island is rich with history and culture and is surrounded by turquoise seas, dotted with dhow masts in the distance. The only means of transport on the island are by foot or donkey. A small channel separates the island from the mainland. Lamu has warm friendly people and features diverse accommodation. The Swahili architecture, fishing villages, mosques and winding streets in the old fashioned stone town, rolling dunes and endless beaches make Lamu a true gem. Visitors can also experience culture through the annual festivals that take place throughout the year including, the Lamu cultural festival, Lamu Art festival and the Lamu yoga festival.
