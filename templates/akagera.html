Akagera National Park

Akagera National park is nestled in the east of the country, bordering Tanzania. The relatively warm and low-lying plains of Akagera comprise savannah, woodland, wetland and a dozen lakes, the largely open expanse is ideal for game viewing. A family of lions from South Africa is settling in well, and breeding successfully, and 18 eastern black rhinos have also been reintroduced. Visitors can also encounter buffalo, elephant, antelope, zebra, giraffe, baboons, monkeys and an incredible 490 bird species. A boat trip on Lake Ihema is also a highlight of any visit to Akagera, with its large pods of hippos, Nile crocodiles and abundant water birds on the island in the middle of the lake. The only way to explore the park is by vehicle.



Here are some of the activities that you can do while in Akagera

  • Safari Drives: Explore the park's diverse habitats and encounter a wide variety of wildlife, including elephants, lions, giraffes, zebras, buffaloes, and more, on thrilling safari drives led by experienced guides.
  • Boat Safaris: Cruise along the picturesque lakes and waterways of Akagera on a boat safari, where you can observe hippos, crocodiles, water birds, and other aquatic creatures up close while enjoying stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Bird Watching: With over 500 bird species recorded in the park, birdwatching enthusiasts will be delighted by the opportunity to spot a diverse array of avian species, including African fish eagles, shoebills, herons, and countless migratory birds.
  • Nature Walks: Embark on guided nature walks through the park's scenic trails, where you can immerse yourself in the sights and sounds of the wilderness, learn about the local flora and fauna, and enjoy breathtaking views of the landscapes.
  • Cultural Visits: Experience the rich cultural heritage of Rwanda by visiting nearby communities and engaging in cultural activities such as traditional dances, craft demonstrations, and visits to local markets, where you can interact with local artisans and learn about their traditional way of life.
  • Fishing: Cast your line into the park's lakes and rivers and try your hand at fishing for tilapia, catfish, and other freshwater species. Fishing excursions can be arranged with local guides, offering a unique way to experience Akagera's natural beauty.
  • Photography Safaris: Capture stunning photographs of Akagera's wildlife and landscapes on guided photography safaris led by experienced photographers who will help you find the best vantage points and lighting conditions for memorable shots.
  • Conservation Tours: Learn about the park's conservation efforts and initiatives aimed at protecting its precious ecosystems and wildlife on educational tours led by park rangers. Gain insights into the challenges and successes of conservation in Akagera.

Wildlife in Akagera

Akagera National Park is home to a diverse array of wildlife, making it a prime destination for safari enthusiasts and nature lovers. Here are some of the notable wildlife species you can encounter while visiting the park:

  • African Elephants: Akagera is home to a thriving population of African elephants, one of the park's most iconic species. These majestic creatures can often be seen roaming the savannah and woodlands in search of food and water.
  • Lions: Encounter the kings of the savannah in Akagera, where lions roam freely across the park's vast landscapes. Witness their majestic presence as they rest in the shade or embark on hunts in search of prey.
  • Leopards: While more elusive than lions, leopards also inhabit Akagera National Park. These solitary predators are expert climbers and may be spotted resting in the branches of trees or stalking their prey under the cover of darkness.
  • Giraffes: Spot graceful giraffes grazing on the park's plains, their elegant silhouettes contrasting against the backdrop of the landscape. Watch as they browse on acacia trees and mingle with other wildlife.
  • Hippos: Observe hippos wallowing in the shallows of Akagera's lakes and rivers, particularly during the dry season when water levels are lower. These massive mammals spend their days submerged in water to stay cool and emerge at night to graze on land.
  • Buffaloes: Encounter herds of African buffalo roaming the park's grasslands and woodlands, often found near water sources where they gather to drink and wallow. These powerful herbivores play a vital role in shaping Akagera's ecosystem.
  • Antelopes: Akagera is home to a variety of antelope species, including impalas, waterbucks, and bushbucks. Watch as these agile herbivores graze on the park's grasslands and seek shelter in the thickets.
  • Crocodiles: Akagera's lakes and rivers are inhabited by Nile crocodiles, formidable predators that lurk beneath the surface, waiting for unsuspecting prey to approach. Witness their stealthy movements and powerful jaws up close.
  • Birdlife: With over 500 bird species recorded, Akagera is a haven for birdwatchers, boasting an impressive array of resident and migratory birds. From colorful kingfishers to majestic African fish eagles, bird enthusiasts will be delighted by the park's avian diversity.

Best Time to Visit

Plan your visit to Akagera National Park wisely:

  • Dry Season (June to September): The dry season offers excellent wildlife viewing opportunities, as animals congregate around water sources, making them easier to spot. Vegetation is also less dense during this time, providing clearer sightlines for wildlife sightings and photography.
  • Wet Season (October to May): The wet season brings lush green landscapes and newborn wildlife, but wildlife sightings may be more challenging due to thicker vegetation. However, it's an ideal time for birdwatching, as migratory birds flock to the park.
  • Shoulder Seasons (June, October, and November): These transitional months offer a balance between the dry and wet seasons, with fewer tourists and pleasant weather conditions. It's a good time to visit for those seeking quieter experiences.
