Amboseli National Park – Land of Giants

Located in southern Kenya, Amboseli National Park is famous for its stunning views of Mount Kilimanjaro, Africa’s highest peak. The park offers a diverse range of wildlife, including large herds of elephants, as well as opportunities for birdwatching, photography, and cultural experiences with the Maasai people.

Activities at Amboseli National Park

  • Game Drives: Explore the park's vast savannahs and swamps on guided safari game drives to spot elephants, lions, cheetahs, buffalo, and more.
  • Nature Walks: Take guided nature walks to discover the park's diverse flora and fauna up close.
  • Birdwatching: With over 400 bird species, Amboseli is a birdwatcher's paradise.
  • Cultural Visits: Experience Maasai culture with visits to local villages and traditional homesteads.
  • Picnicking: Enjoy a picnic amidst the scenic landscapes of Amboseli.
  • Photography: Capture breathtaking views of Mount Kilimanjaro and wildlife in their natural habitat.
  • Sundowner Safaris: Witness stunning sunsets over the plains during evening game drives.

Wildlife

  • Elephants: Amboseli is renowned for its large herds of elephants, often seen against the backdrop of Mount Kilimanjaro.
  • Lions: Spot prides of lions lounging in the shade or on the hunt.
  • Cheetahs: Watch these sleek predators as they roam the open plains.
  • Giraffes: Observe giraffes grazing on acacia trees scattered across the park.
  • Buffalo: Encounter herds of Cape buffalo as they roam the grasslands.
  • Zebras: Capture images of zebras against the backdrop of Kilimanjaro.
  • Wildebeests: Witness the great migration as wildebeests move through the park.
  • Rhinos: Although rare, both black and white rhinos can be found in Amboseli.

Best Time to Visit

The dry season (July to October) is the best time for wildlife viewing, as animals gather around water sources, and vegetation is sparse. However, Amboseli is a year-round destination, with different experiences to offer in each season.
