Kenya

Kenya, located in East Africa, is renowned for its diverse landscapes, abundant wildlife, and rich cultural heritage. From the iconic Maasai Mara National Reserve to the pristine beaches of the Kenyan coast, Kenya offers a wealth of experiences for travelers. Embark on a safari adventure in the Maasai Mara, where you can witness the Great Migration, an awe-inspiring spectacle of millions of wildebeest, zebras, and other animals crossing the plains in search of greener pastures.
Explore the Amboseli National Park, known for its large herds of elephants and stunning views of Mount Kilimanjaro, Africa's highest peak. For cultural immersion, visit traditional Maasai villages and learn about their fascinating way of life. Discover the vibrant culture and history of coastal cities like Mombasa and Lamu, known for their Swahili architecture, bustling markets, and rich maritime heritage.
Explore the Rift Valley region, home to scenic landscapes, lakes, and national parks such as Lake Nakuru National Park, famous for its flamingos, and Hell's Gate National Park, where you can hike, rock climb, and enjoy stunning geothermal features. Kenya's coastline offers pristine beaches and marine parks, including Diani Beach and Watamu Marine National Park, where you can snorkel, dive, and relax on white sandy beaches. With its diverse attractions and warm hospitality, Kenya promises unforgettable experiences for adventurers, nature lovers, and culture enthusiasts alike.


Uganda

Uganda also known as the “The Pearl of Africa” is a lush and green country in East Africa that has so much to offer. Its diverse landscape encompasses the snow-capped “Mountains of the Moon”, the Rwenzori Mountains and the continent’s largest fresh water lake, Lake Victoria, known as the “Source of the Nile”.
It also has many different species of wildlife including habituation of chimpanzees and “wild chimps” as well as many rare bird species. It truly is a “birder’s paradise” ! There are 10 gazetted National Parks which are all quite varied, ranging from those located near / within the large water bodies including the mighty “NILE” to Savannah type of vegetation in the Kidepo National Park and the evergreen Rainforests boasting various primates with the most popular ones being Gorillas and Chimpanzees. Kidepo is the only park that hosts cheetahs. The Bwindi Impenetrable National Park is renowned for the endangered Mountain Gorilla, one of the last refuges for these beautiful giants in their natural habitat. A visit to Murchison Falls National Park with 43m-tall waterfall of River Nile is a must whilst visiting. Enjoy a river cruise to view hippos up close and enjoy this spectacular waterfall between massive rocks. Uganda is known for its diverse culture, warm hospitality and freshest organic tropical fruit you can eat.


Tanzania

Tanzania, located in East Africa, is a land of breathtaking landscapes and diverse wildlife. Home to iconic destinations such as the Serengeti National Park, known for its vast plains and the Great Migration, and Mount Kilimanjaro, Africa's highest peak offering adventurous trekking experiences, Tanzania has something for every traveler.
Explore the Serengeti's endless savannahs teeming with wildlife, including lions, elephants, and giraffes, and witness the awe-inspiring spectacle of the Great Migration, where millions of wildebeest and other animals cross the plains in search of greener pastures.
For nature lovers, the Ngorongoro Crater, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, offers an unparalleled wildlife viewing experience within its ancient volcanic caldera. Discover the rich biodiversity of the tropical rainforests in Gombe Stream National Park and Mahale Mountains National Park, home to chimpanzees and other primates.
Tanzania's coastline along the Indian Ocean is dotted with idyllic islands and pristine beaches, such as Zanzibar and Mafia Island, where you can relax on white sandy beaches, snorkel in crystal-clear waters, and immerse yourself in the island's rich cultural heritage.
With its diverse landscapes, abundant wildlife, and warm hospitality, Tanzania promises unforgettable experiences for travelers seeking adventure, relaxation, and cultural immersion.


Rwanda

Known as ‘The Land of a Thousand Hills’, Rwanda has so much to offer from its magnificent scenery to unique experiences. Rwanda’s people are warm & humble and you won’t forget this beautiful country, nor its people ! But the most fascinating inhabitants of this land, are one third of the world’s remaining population of endangered Mountain Gorillas, the rarest of the three gorilla sub-species.
People from all over the world come to catch a glimpse of the magnificent gorillas and golden monkeys. There are only about 820 gorillas remaining in the Wild. There is no doubt that this is an experience you will be forever etched in your memory.
One of the advantages of visiting Rwanda is the close proximity of all the main attractions. Most are located between one and four hours from the capital city of Kigali. From rainforests and volcanoes at the Volcanoes National Park & Nyungwe Forest National Park, to the low-lying savannah of the Akagera National Park to the forests of Nyungwe National Park and beautiful Lake Kivu.
You may wish to visit the lush and green Nyungwe Forest, home to many species of chimpanzees and Colobus monkeys. The Canopy Walkway offers stunning views over the valley and forests. Visit the Kamiranzovu Marsh, with its birdlife, orchids and waterfall.


1. Best safari destination?
- Some of the best safari destinations in East Africa include the Serengeti National Park in Tanzania and the Maasai Mara National Reserve in Kenya. These areas are renowned for their incredible wildlife sightings, including the Great Migration, where millions of animals traverse the plains in search of greener pastures.
2. Best time to visit East Africa?
- The best time to visit East Africa depends on your interests. Generally, the dry season (June to October) is ideal for wildlife viewing as animals gather around water sources. However, the wet season (November to May) offers lush landscapes, fewer crowds, and the chance to witness the Great Migration in Tanzania's Serengeti or Kenya's Maasai Mara.
3. Popular places to visit in East Africa?
- Explore iconic destinations like Maasai Mara National Reserve, Serengeti National Park, Zanzibar, Mount Kilimanjaro, Bwindi Impenetrable National Park, Victoria Falls, and Ngorongoro Conservation Area for diverse wildlife, stunning beaches, majestic waterfalls, and thrilling trekking experiences.
4. Top national parks and reserves in East Africa?
- Some of the top national parks and reserves in East Africa include Serengeti National Park and Ngorongoro Conservation Area in Tanzania, Maasai Mara National Reserve and Amboseli National Park in Kenya, and Bwindi Impenetrable National Park and Queen Elizabeth National Park in Uganda.
5. Best places to visit in Kenya?
- Kenya offers a diverse range of attractions, including Maasai Mara National Reserve for exceptional wildlife viewing, Amboseli National Park for stunning views of Mount Kilimanjaro and large herds of elephants, and Diani Beach for its white sandy beaches and water sports activities.
6. Best places to visit in Tanzania?
- Tanzania is home to remarkable destinations such as Serengeti National Park, renowned for its vast plains and the Great Migration, Mount Kilimanjaro, offering unforgettable trekking experiences, and Zanzibar, an idyllic island paradise with beautiful beaches, rich history, and vibrant culture.
