Nyungwe Forest

Nyungwe Forest national park is home to 25% of all of Africa's primates, including a large population of chimpanzees, which produce the national park's main attraction: chimpanzee trekking. Set in the Southwest of the country, this is one of the oldest rainforests in Africa, Rich in biodiversity, with regular rainfalls of up to 2,000mm per annum. The region is teaming with wildlife, including a healthy number of Ruwenzori Colobus monkeys, Hoest’s monkey, chimpanzee, silver monkey, golden monkey, olive baboon, Vervet monkey, several species of bush baby, grey-cheeked mangabey and red-tailed monkey. The forest is situated in a region where several large-scale bio-geographical zones meet and provide a great span of micro-habitats for many different species of plants and animals. The park contains 13 different primate species, over 300 bird species, 1068 plant species, 85 mammal species, 32 amphibian and 38 reptile species. Botanists will marvel at the plant species and over 140 orchids found in Nyungwe. The bird watching is among the best in Africa with over 300 species including the Red-collared Babbler and 29 other Albertine Rift Endemic species. With 15 trails and various other activities, visitors will be spoilt for choice by what this forest has to offer.
Memorable and photogenic moments include walking up to the Isumo waterfall or along the Canopy Walk suspension bridge. The 160m long and 70m high suspension bridge is accessible as part of a guided tour and is only advisable for children older than 6 years old. This is East Africa’s highest canopy walk. This park is undoubtedly one of the country’s main attractions with waterfalls, abundant wildlife and viewpoints over the tranquil scenery.
Along with its biodiversity, Nyungwe is an important water catchment for Rwanda and contains many natural resources integral to Rwanda’s human populations.

Activities in Nyungwe National Park

  • Chimpanzee Tracking: Embark on guided treks through the forest to observe chimpanzees in their natural habitat.
  • Canopy Walk: Experience the thrill of walking on suspension bridges high above the forest canopy, offering panoramic views of the park.
  • Bird Watching: Nyungwe is a paradise for birdwatchers, with over 300 bird species, including the colorful turacos and rare Albertine Rift endemics.
  • Hiking Trails: Explore a network of hiking trails that lead to scenic viewpoints, waterfalls, and diverse habitats within the park.
  • Primate Encounters: Besides chimpanzees, Nyungwe is home to other primate species such as colobus monkeys, vervet monkeys, and L'Hoest's monkeys.
  • Cultural Experiences: Visit nearby communities to learn about the culture and traditions of the local people, including the Banyarwanda and Batwa pygmies.

Wildlife and Nature

Nyungwe National Park boasts a rich biodiversity, with an abundance of flora and fauna. In addition to primates and birds, the park is home to various mammal species, including duikers, bushbucks, and civets.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Nyungwe National Park is during the dry season, from June to September, when the weather is favorable for trekking and wildlife viewing. However, the park can be visited year-round, with each season offering unique experiences.
